Thread Of Peace

What is peace?
A quiet sky?
A life without storms?

Or is it holding someone’s hand
while the world falls apart —
and choosing them anyway?

They said,
“I can’t find peace with you.”

But I was ready to burn
just to be the eclipse
that let our light meet,
even if only for a moment.

Pain came like waves,
yes — but I never saw it
as greater than love.

Even when it hurt,
I believed
peace was the hush of her name
echoing under a starry night.

She left —
chasing a softer stillness.

She thought the shadows
meant the love was wrong.
I thought the shadows
were just what made the stars
shine brighter.

Since then, I ask myself:
Is peace the absence of pain?
Or the presence of someone
who makes the pain
feel like part of the sky?

We fight with family,
we ache for them —
but we don’t walk away.
Because love is louder
than silence.

So I wonder…
Did I lose her
to a different kind of peace?
Or did I find
a quiet truth among the stars:

That peace wears different faces.
And sometimes,
we become the eclipse
for the Moon we love —
not to block her glow,
but to meet her in the only moment
the sky allowed us.

Still,
if I could go back —
I would choose her again.
Choose the ache,
the gravity,
the starless hours.

Because peace to me
was never perfect silence —
It was always
just her glow
in my orbit.

And maybe…
if I was the Sun,
she was the Moon,
then in some lifetime
she was also my sunflower —
turning toward the light,
even when it burned.

Now…
I am Sol.
I am Solastial —
shining in silence,
knowing my Moon
will never come back.
