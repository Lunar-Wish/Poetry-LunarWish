The Cage and the Sky

I’m not here to give answers.
I’m just searching, same as you —
with paint on my hands and questions in my chest.

There’s this white bird I drew.
It sits at the open door of its cage.
The sky is calling,
the world is wide open…
and still,
it stays.

Maybe you’d call it foolish.
But maybe —
maybe it knows something we don’t.
We always talk like freedom means flying,
like wings only matter when they’re chasing clouds.

But what if that bird remembers
the soft mornings inside those walls?
The quiet safety?
The feeling of being held?
Maybe the cage isn’t chains at all.
Maybe it’s just… home.

We call lines and walls prisons.
But sometimes, they’re shields.
Sometimes, they’re the arms that hold us close.
A voice that knows who we are.

I painted another piece too —
a tiny locked cage, floating out in the middle of the stars.
It’s fragile, small, surrounded by galaxies.
And I thought… isn’t that us?

This little planet in an endless universe.
We build walls here too —
not just to keep people out,
but to keep love in.
To know where we belong.
To have a place that means something.

But still,
I get it.
To some people, even soft walls feel heavy.
Even the people who love you most
can feel like bars.

That’s what my music tries to say —
It drifts between sky and ground,
between running away
and staying close.
Between wanting to fly
and wanting to stay wrapped in something warm.

Maybe peace isn’t always breaking free.
Maybe it’s just choosing —
choosing to stay,
choosing to love,
choosing to breathe deeply
where it feels right.

And I guess I’ll always wonder…
Is it really a cage,
if your heart quietly says —
"I feel safe here"?
