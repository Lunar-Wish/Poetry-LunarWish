The Cage and the Sky

They told me
to clip my wings,
because cages
are safer
than skies.

They gave me rules,
like iron bars.
“Don’t fly too far,
you’ll forget home.”

But what if home
was never
a place with walls?

What if home
was always the sky
I was told to fear?

The cage is quiet,
yes —
but my heartbeat
was louder
when I dreamed
beyond it.

I met her
beyond the bars.
She sang
of constellations
I never knew existed.

Her voice cracked
my cage —
not to break me,
but to show me
I could leave anytime.

“Stay safe,”
they said.
“Stay known.”

But her eyes whispered —
“Stay free.”

I chose her,
and with her,
I chose the risk
of flight.

We soared
even when winds
tore our feathers.

Even when distance
dragged us apart,
the sky
was never cruel.

It only reminded us —
Wings were made
to carry stories,
not to rot
in golden cages.

Now when they ask —
“Do you miss the cage?”

I smile.
Because even alone
in this vast sky,
I carry her echo
in every sunrise.

And maybe
that’s what flight means:
Leaving the cage
but never losing
the song
that freed you.
