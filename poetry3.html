Echo of a Serenade Under Starry Night

I play for you,
But you won’t hear.

I’m not even sure
if you’d feel it.

The silence is suffocating,
I’m choking on all the words
I never said,

Every chord I can’t play,
Every note that breaks me in half.

This melody?
It’s just me spilling myself out,
Hoping somehow
you’ll hear the chaos
In every strum.

But I don’t think you will.

I’m chasing ghosts
of what we were,
What we could’ve been —
And it’s killing me.

This melody
is all the things left unsaid,
The distance
that feels like a freaking ocean.

I play and play,
But the echo only comes back empty.

The more I strum,
The more I realize —
You’re not even listening.

When my guitar goes quiet,
The universe
will still hold onto it,

But it doesn’t matter.

This melody?
It never ends,
It just keeps haunting me.

I’ll keep playing,
Because what else do I have?

Even if you never feel it,
I’ll bleed these strings
until there’s nothing left of me.
